Project KALPIT

Digital Creation to Enable Gamification Based on Indian Ethos

Project KALPIT is a prestigious sponsored research initiative supported by the Department of Science and Technology (DST) under the SERB-INAE (Science and Engineering Research Board – Indian National Academy of Engineering) programme's "Online and Digital Gaming Research Initiative". Led by Principal Investigator Prof. Uma Mudenagudi, the project is run at the KLETech Center for Visual Intelligence (CEVI).

Official Project Page: cevi.co.in/projects/sponsored/kalpit

Overview of the digital asset generation and virtual interaction pipelines under Project KALPIT.

Core Objective

The overarching mission of Project KALPIT is to preserve, digitize, and gamify India's rich cultural heritage, historical artifacts, traditional designs, and storytelling traditions. By combining advanced computer vision, computer graphics, and generative AI pipelines, the project builds a high-fidelity 3D ecosystem. This enables immersive digital experiences—ranging from educational games to spatial reality/AR applications—rooted in Indian ethos and values.

Key Pillars of my Contribution

As a Junior Research Fellow and Scholar at CEVI closely working on KALPIT, my research focuses on the core visual intelligence blocks that power the digital creation pipeline:

3D Asset Generation (ShilpAI)

Developed generative AI tools utilizing models like TRELLIS and Hunyuan3D-2.1 to reconstruct accurate 3D geometries and synthesize PBR materials for cultural artifacts and traditional jewelry (Jhumkas, rings) from single-view photographs.

View ShilpAI →

4D Human Digitization (SPiRF4Humans)

Created spatio-temporal refinement frameworks for dynamic 4D point clouds of humans, enabling realistic reconstruction of moving characters and avatars for games and XR stages.

View SPiRF4Humans →

Expressive 3D Reconstructions (Nritya3D)

Researched monocular video-driven expressive 3D human mesh reconstruction, enabling skeletal and facial gesture transfer to cultural avatars.

Interactive 3D Showcase

Created interactive galleries and web-based AR pipelines to host, view, and test reconstructed assets in real-time, bringing traditional assets to life.

Technical Impact

By transitioning from manual 3D modeling to AI-driven generation and volumetric capture, Project KALPIT demonstrates how low-cost, high-speed digitalization can scale. These generated assets populate digital twins of historical sites, allow real-time virtual try-ons for traditional jewelry, and serve as game assets in interactive experiences that promote Indian heritage.
